Why I Built This

It was 2 AM. I was four hours deep into debugging a production issue. My eyes were burning, my neck was stiff, and I hadn't blinked in what felt like forever.

I only noticed when my vision started blurring.

Sound familiar? We developers are notorious for losing ourselves in code. "Just one more fix." "Almost got it." Before you know it, hours have passed and your body is screaming at you.

So I built AFK. Not another fancy productivity app. Just a simple reminder to step away, look at something 20 feet away, and give your eyes a break.

Now I take a 30-second break every 25 minutes. My eyes don't hurt anymore. And honestly? I code better. Turns out the breaks I resisted were making me slower.

— Harry, the developer behind AFK

Get Started in 60 Seconds

1

Download & Install

Option A: Direct download

Download DMG

Open the DMG and drag Afk to Applications.

Option B: Via Homebrew

brew tap Harry-kp/tap && brew install --cask afk

Don't have Homebrew? Get it at brew.sh

2

First Launch

macOS will ask for permission since AFK isn't from the App Store. Just right-click the app → Open → Open. You only need to do this once.

3

Start a Session

Click the 👀 icon in your menu bar → Start Session. Or open the app and hit the golden button. The timer starts counting down.

4

Take Your Breaks

When time's up, a fullscreen reminder appears. Look away from the screen. Blink a few times. Stretch if you want. 30 seconds later, you're back to work.

The 20-20-20 Rule

Eye doctors recommend this simple rule: Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds.

It helps prevent digital eye strain, reduces dry eyes, and keeps your focus sharp. The problem? Nobody actually does it.

AFK makes it automatic. You don't have to remember. You don't have to set timers. Just work, and AFK will remind you when it's time to look away.
